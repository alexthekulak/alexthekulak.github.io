Minimum Wage: Some Thoughts

There’s been a lot of talk recently about the minimum wage in the US, and for good reason- the CBO released a new report on the Raise the Wage Act of 2021 that I highly recommend everyone take a look at. I will also be the first to admit that I’m neither a labor economist nor a macroeconomist more generally, but I wanted to add a few comments to the discussion on a variety of matters.

First and foremost, it’s not just that economists aren’t sure about the effect of raising the minimum wage- we’re not even sure how to talk about our uncertainty. I think this recent paper by David Neumark and Peter Shirley describes the current (lack of) consensus well. I also have no particular issue with their conclusions: seems likely that there is going to be (somewhat) weak impacts negatively and this will probably affect teenagers and young adults most of all.
